File truncation, saving data & NAS explained - training course

The Research Computing team have developed a short training course to help staff understand where to save data, and how to truncate and retrieve files from a lab, project, or home share.

The objective of the course is to teach good housekeeping practices when saving and retrieving data. By following best practices, lab/project share quotas should maintain a healthy ebb and flow of data, ensuring space is always available for saving research.

Course content

The key topics include:

  • a short overview of WEHI's central storage - so users will understand where to save different data
  • NAS - the new central storage that replaced fs_all and prkfs
  • what is a lab/project share
  • what is a quota and how do I check my quota
  • how to truncate files & folders
  • how to retrieve truncated files & folders
  • what to do if you encounter an error whilst truncating/retrieving files
  • a hands-on practice at the end of the session for those who want to test their new skills whilst a trainer is available to help.

     

Intended audience

The course is open to any WEHI staff members who use lab, home and project shares.

Course length

The course duration is one hour (or 45mins if the hands-on practice is not required).

Course availability

The course can be booked any time, for any number of students.
