Disk quotas

Some of WEHI's filesystems are managed by quotas. This is to prevent emergency truncation events from occurring which are disruptive to all working systems, as well as researchers, and research, at the Institute.

Filesystems that have quotas enforced are as follows:

FileSystemQuota
Home20 GB/user
VAST ProjectsAs per allocation
Lab/Projects shares on GeneralAs per allocation
ProjectsAs per allocation

How do I manage my quota on Home?

  • ​​​​​​​To free up space in your quota, simply truncate the files you don't currently require on disk.
  • When you require them again, you can easily retrieve them.
  • You may truncate and retrieve files as many times as required.​
  • WEHI offer a free training course about WEHI Central Storage, where to save data & how to truncate files. Contact the Helpdesk to make a booking or go to the File Truncation Training page to view the course contents and learn more about the course.

Mac OSX Users

Depending on which version of Mac OS you run, you will either see 'Offline Actions' or 'Services' (or both):​​​​​​​

Offline Actions

  • Right-click on the file or folder, and select 'Offline Actions', then choose to retrieve, truncate or store content.
  • If you do not see this option, please contact the ITS Helpdesk with your Mac number and request that the Stornext client is installed.
  • Please review this short video (2m:29s) from Stornext which explains how, as well as what, the new icons look like and what they mean.

Services

  • Right-click on the file or folder, and select 'Services', to choose from either: WEHI Stornext truncate, status, or retrieve​.
  • Please refer to How To Retrieve a Truncated File (macOS) for more detailed instructions.

Windows Users

  • Right-click on the file or folder, and hover over 'WEHI Services', to choose to either retrieve or truncate the file or folder.
  • Please refer to How To Retrieve a Truncated File (Windows 10) ​for more detailed instructions.​
  • If you do not see this option, please contact the ITS Helpdesk with your PC number.
    ​​​​​​​

Linux users

  • Access to the Stornext module gives you access to commands snfileinfo, snretrieve, and snrmdiskcopy (truncate).
  • Please view the attached PowerPoint presentation StornextToolsLinux.pdf
  • If you do not have access to the Stornext module, please contact  support@wehi.edu.au with your machine name and/or number​.

How do I request an increase to my home quota?

If you require an increase to your home quota, please submit your request to the ITS Helpdesk including your reason.

Each request will be considered on a case-by-case basis.​​​​​​​​​ However, you will have to demonstrate that you've exhausted all other filesystem alternatives.
