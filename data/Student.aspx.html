Introduction

 

The RCP Discovery Program is an initiative to encourage researchers across WEHI to engage with RCP in student internship projects to help discover and prototype new ideas.  

 

The program aims to help WEHI researchers understand and uncover new ideas' complexity and help mature them while working with student interns interested in Data Science and Software Engineering. 

 

Background

 

The WEHI Research Computing Platform regularly provides student internship opportunities through the University of Melbourne Science and Engineering programs to help find and nurture talented Research Software Engineers (RSEs). These internships are 100% remote, with the Science interns averaging 1 day a week over 3 months, and the Engineering students averaging 3 days a week over 3 months.  

  

Many projects work in data analysis and research software engineering using high-performance computing (HPC). We work across diverse projects such as imaging, cryo-EM, genomics, transcriptomics, health informatics, and capacity planning.  

 

Some projects have spanned multiple intakes which has allowed these projects to mature their documentation and onboarding and to reduce the learning curve for new students. 

  

We mainly work with projects that use R and Shiny, Python, Julia, bash, while also making the most out of other technologies such as RStudio, Jupyter notebooks, PowerBI and other applications within the data analytics space. 

 

Benefits for Researchers 

 

Some projects that have benefited from working with students include:

  • exploring approaches and architectural designs to expose the complexity of difficult problems - like managing different types of research data,

  • developing user stories, wireframes, and proof-of-concept software to provide a clearer vision of what a good solution might look like to researchers and research software engineers (RSEs),

  • setting up onboarding and technical documentation, testing or improving infrastructure like containers to help new people use research software or research workflows, and

  • exploring new avenues of technology and sharing documentation and suggested approaches to learning, like the Quantum Computing project.

 

This can help bridge the gap to complementary programs such as the RCP Capability Building program, which works on mature projects. The Discovery program is a potential pathway to philanthropic or grant funding. 

 

 

 

Researcher Expectations 

 

Results may vary for projects over a single intake. Over a series of intakes, the outcomes a researcher could expect to see from the Discovery program would include: 

  • a public wiki of high-level knowledge of the domain to help students, Research Software Engineers (RSEs) and possibly new lab members to learn the domain quickly 

  • an internal SharePoint of concrete, practical knowledge of the domain within WEHI to help students, RSEs and possibly new lab members to learn the domain quickly 

  • a set of publicly available raw datasets, workflows, and processed datasets to help students and RSEs have a concrete understanding of research workflows,  

  • one or many presentations on the complexities uncovered by the students and potential theoretical recommendations, and 

  • one or many repositories showing concrete proof-of-concepts that were built during one or more intakes. 

 

 

RCP Expectations 

 

As RCP has limited resources, we will not be able to commence on all project ideas that will be submitted. Some projects might be postponed to the next round based on the selection criteria. It is expected that three Discovery projects per intake would be taken on by RCP. 

 

For the project to succeed the RCP needs: 

  • a willingness from researchers to help share high-level concepts that can be publicly documented and synthesised by students to share with future students, 

  • a willingness for researchers to help identify publicly available raw data, workflows, and processed data to have as a baseline of technical knowledge and subject matter expertise,  

  • an opportunity for students to present at a lab meeting or divisional seminar, depending on the quality of their work, 

  • a recognition that students are a “best-effort” approach that may have to span multiple intakes to achieve practical benefits to researchers and, 

  • flexibility to allow students to self-direct while staying within the broad project idea.

 

 

 Submitting and Prioritising Ideas 

 

Submitted ideas should be broad to allow students to help uncover complexities within a 3-month (single intake) or 6-month (over two intakes) timespan.  At the end of the first intake, an assessment will be made with the researcher and RCP to decide if another intake would be worthwhile. 

 

It is hoped that if the idea shows merit the supervisor could submit a Capability Building Program Expression of Interest.  

 

The RCP Strategic Engagement Manager will provide recommendations on the priority of all appropriate projects. The recommendations will be reviewed by the RCP Leadership team to provide oversight. 

 

 

Expression of Interest Process and EOI Form

 

The overall process for applying is as follows.  

  

  • Submit an Expression of Interest via this form.  You can see the form information described below. 

  • There may be an email or meeting to clarify the project with the RCP Strategic Engagement Manager 

  • If the submission is successful, a supervisor from RCP will be allocated to work on the project 

 

Submissions happen 3 times per year for the Semester 1, Semester 2, and Summer intakes. 

 

 

Example of an EOI
 

An example of a submitted EOI is as follows: 

  • Title (required)

  • Problem description, including challenges you or others face (Required)

  • Future blue-sky vision (Optional)

  • Benefits to the WEHI community (Optional)

  • Extra information (Optional) 

 

Title: Data Commons 

Problem Description: How can we connect related datasets for collaborators and lab members to make it easier to find raw, processed, and summarised data?  

Blue sky vision: A great long-term outcome would be that a researcher can search for a selection of datasets within a theme (eg. Pancreatic Cancer), find an interesting dataset and start loading it into R Studio within 5 minutes if they have access.  

Benefits to the WEHI community: This would allow other labs to suggest and contribute to a body of knowledge that could help us find ways to streamline our research data management across collaborations and labs for specific themes. 

Extra information: We know that Institute XYZ was also keen on collaborating with this. It would be nice to explore the possibilities around this. We could provide test datasets that are publicly available to use as benchmarks. 

 

 

Selection Criteria 

 

The selection criteria for the prioritisation of the Discovery project ideas are the following: 

 

  1. In the future, could this project support a strategic priority at WEHI?  

  2. In the future, could this idea/project develop or create new computational or data analytics capabilities?   

  3. In the future, could this help enable multi-disciplinary research or futuristic research? 

  4. In the future, could this support research within multiple labs/groups at WEHI? 

  5. In the future, could this help raise awareness of the value of Research Software Engineers (RSEs) at WEHI?  

 

 

Other options for student interns 

Please note that researchers who would be happy to fully supervise students can approach RCP for help recruiting students. This includes the researchers interviewing the students while RCP can help with advertising and onboarding.
