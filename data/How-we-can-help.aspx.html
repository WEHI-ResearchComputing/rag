How can the Research Computing teams help you?..
​​​​​​​

Firstly, if you're not sure, just ask!

We are a friendly bunch who will always happily help with any questions you may have..

We can help you in a multitude of ways, including (but not limited to):

Platforms:

  • What systems to use for computational requirements
  • Workflows
  • Physical infrastructure (storage, compute & network performance, or other operational issues)
  • Remote access to resources
  • Virtual machines & virtual desktops
  • Optimising computing power to best suit the needs of different research workloads
  • GPU/Visualisation workloads
  • Accessing cloud

Software:

  • Deployment on Milton/Linux
  • Developing software solutions
  • Software engineering advice

Applications:

  • RStudio
  • Shiny
  • RedCap
  • Spotfire
  • CryoEM applications ( Relion, Cryosparc etc)
  • Imaging applications


    Anything data management related:

    • Answering questions about lab/project shares & tasks (creating, moving, merging, adding/removing users)
    • Data reporting and oversight
    • Quota management & increase requests
    • File truncation & retrieval
    • Deploying data for publications/projects

    Training:

    • Developing custom training programs
    • Developing guides and documentation
    • Delivering training programs remotely, online or face-to-face

    HPC scheduler/resource manager areas:

    • Explaining SLURM, how it is configured & any new features required
    • What is needed to run new jobs
    • Managing & submitting jobs
    • Checking the progress of jobs
    • Understand why jobs do & don’t run
    • Reporting job resource consumption & how efficiently the resources are used​​​​​​​

      Collaboration:

      • Engage & foster the development of digital communities
      • Support networks of informatics across the Institute
      • Provide guidance to researchers & help exploit the infrastructure to their best advantage
      • Build compute, data, digital methods competencies within scientific computing
      • Actively build digital literacy across WEHI
      • Improve efficiency & productivity for scientists by allowing them to access & generate computational outputs with reduced time & effort
      • Support researchers’ access to local and national computational & bioinformatic platforms
        ​​​​​​​

      Management areas:

      • Any overarching team questions? Would you like someone to help with something bigger than a helpdesk ticket?
      • Advice about external resources
      • Help writing requirements input into uplift projects/spend, grant applications etc
      • Talk to RC first when specifying instruments uplifts or new projects – we provide help with estimating, budgeting for disk/tape usage, compute (CPU, GPU, RAM) usage
      • Guidance for creating data management policies/SOPS

      If you don't see what you're looking for on this list, again, just ask! Our work and remit is constantly evolving as we continuously look for ways to improve and grow.

      Email research.computing@wehi.edu.au and we will get back to you to discuss your needs in more detail.
