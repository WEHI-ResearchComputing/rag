Contact us


Contact the Research Computing team by:

  • emailing us at research.computing@wehi.edu.au
  • raising a ticket with the Helpdesk at support@wehi.edu.au, or calling them on x2589


Your email or helpdesk ticket will be analysed and assigned to the appropriate technician within the team. Someone will be in touch with you shortly afterwards.


Getting support

The best mechanism for getting support is through the Helpdesk rather than contacting individuals. This Catalyst document describes the numerous ways to contact the Helpdesk or raise a ticket.

Note: ITS only provide support between 9am-5pm on working days, i.e. business hours.

Helpdesk staff can quickly triage your problem and direct it to the correct team or individual. Tickets also help ITS manage issues that are affecting many users and analyse historical trends.

For urgent problems, ring the Helpdesk on 2589. For less urgent problems raise a ticket by emailing support@wehi.edu.au. If your ticket has not been responded to in a time frame that is reasonable, in your judgement, follow up by replying to the ticket.

When raising a ticket provide as much information as you reasonably can:

  • The software you were using
  • How you were using it, e.g. the exact command
  • The system you were using 
  • What triggered the issue or what was happening when the issue occurred
  • Copy and paste, or attach logs, error messages, and/or screenshots
  • Does this seem similar to an issue you've had in the past?

Ticket workflows

1.You can submit a ticket by emailing support@wehi.edu.au. You will receive an automatic response that includes a link to your ticket in the ticketing system. The link can be used to update and track the ticket process, although you are not required to.
2.Tickets are initially triaged by the Helpdesk. The most important thing for them to know is the system and software causing issues, which allows them to quickly assign the ticket to the correct team.
3.All members of the target team receive an email when the ticket is assigned. Depending on the practices of the team, a member with expertise in the specific issue will pick it up or the ticket will be assigned to a team member.
4The person handling the ticket may fix the issue, request more information, or provide an update indicating that fixing the issue may take time. The issue may require the involvement of other teams or external parties, for example, vendors, which will delay the issue resolution. Depending on the complexity of the issue, there may be an iterative process between support personnel and the user where various solutions are tested or more diagnostic information is gathered.
5When support feels the issue has been solved the ticket status is changed to "resolved" and you are notified by an automatic email. If the issue has not been resolved to your satisfaction respond to the email explaining why.
6If no further action is required the ticket is closed.
