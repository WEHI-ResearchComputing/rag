Sharing data with collaborators (or yourself)

Currently, WEHI does not have self-serve support to share data with collaborators or "pull" data out of WEHI for use elsewhere. This document describes, in broad terms, what current options might look like.

Please contact Research Computing directly or via support@wehi.edu.au to discuss your requirements. This document does not discuss publishing data.

Please Note:
You need to ensure that you are entitled to share your data with collaborators or move it to other sites. Sharing data with collaborators probably needs a Material Transfer Agreement. Please discuss with your lab head or the Business Development Office if unsure.​​​​​​​

Transferring your own data to or from other facilities

If you need to "pull" your data onto another facility, e.g. Spartan, Pawsey, NCI, etc, ITS can provision an sftp (secure file transfer protocol) service for you.

To access your data, you need to log in to the sftp service with your WEHI login and password. For this reason, this mechanism is not suitable for sharing data with collaborators, unless you also have a login at your partner's institution. Sharing your credentials with other parties is a serious breach of the WEHI ITS acceptable use policy.

If you have data on another facility you can probably pull it from there to WEHI using scp. If, for whatever reason, that does not work ITS can provision an sftp service that you can write your data to.

Sharing data with collaborators

There are a number of possibilities for sharing data.

  1. It may be that a partner has an option to push the data directly but possibly won't.
  2. CloudStor is available to anyone with an Australian Access Federation login, which includes Melbourne University. Researchers can share up to 1TB of data.
  3. Via a cloud service such as AWS. This involves one partner provisioning cloud storage and providing the partner with access credentials. For large data transfers, this can be expensive, try to get your partner to pay for it. Contact Research Computing and we can estimate the costs and, if necessary, set up the storage, access credentials, and help transfer data.
  4. Small amounts of data can be shared through One Drive (using your WEHI account) or Melbourne Uni Fig Share.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​

Sharing large amounts of data externally

As mentioned in #2 of the sharing data section (above), the best way at present to share large amounts of data externally is to use Cloudstor at UniMelb.

Every WEHI researcher can sign up for an AAF affiliated email address/login if you don't already have one and Cloudstor can take up to 1TB of data. It is also possible to script, if you wish.

In the future, the Research Data Management team is looking to implement a WEHI-sponsored solution, but this is unlikely to be implemented until some time in 2021 at the earliest.

Using Cloudstor - instructions

AARNET, who currently host Cloudstor, have announced the service will be decommissioned on Friday 15 December 2023, therefore, we recommend its use for short term projects ONLY. For the full announcement see https://support.aarnet.edu.au/hc/en-us/articles/5697089309711-Decommissioning-CloudStor-

There is a CloudStor getting started guide here, written by AARnet. This descibes the browser-based interface which is adequate for small datasets. For large datasets, especially datasets on WEHI central storage the web interface is not usable. The main reason is that the data has to be pulled from storage over the network to your local machine before being uploaded by the browser. This is particularly cumbersome if you are working remotely via a domestic, hotel or airport network. Data can be uploaded as described in this document from AARnet titled 'can I use the command line or WebDav' here.

Rclone is difficult to set up but is probably faster and more reliable, contact Research Computing if you would like help to do this.

WebDav uploads using the CyberDuck command-line tool are easy to start but speed is highly variable and the upload needs to be repeated multiple times until it completes successfully.

From a WEHI Linux machine:

module load duck\nduck -y -u <unimelb email address> -e resume --parallel 8 --upload davs://cloudstor.aarnet.edu.au/plus/remote.php/webdav/<destination folder> <input folder>\n

The first time you will be prompted for your username and password but this is stored for subsequent uploads.
